Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone is America's first and most prominent national park. It's home to wildlife from bears to bison and geological marvels such as hot springs and geysers

Location: Wyoming, Idaho, Montana

Size: 2,221,766 acres

Most popular attraction: Old Faithful

Yosemite National Park

Designated a World Heritage Site in 1984, Yosemite is known for its granite cliffs, waterfalls, clear streams, giant sequoia groves and biological diversity.

Location: California

Size: 747,956 acres

Most popular attraction: Yosemite Falls

Everglades National Park

The Everglades is the largest source of tropical wilderness in the United States. It protects the habitat for numerous rare species like the manatee, American crocodile, and the Florida panther.

Location: Florida

Size: 470,000 acres

Most popular attraction: Anhinga Trail
